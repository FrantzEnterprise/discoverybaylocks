Paws and Effect: Hilarious Pet Lock Adventures from Real Locksmiths

June 30, 2026 • Discovery Locks and More

🐾 Because Fido Shouldn't Have the Key to Your Liquor Cabinet

After 20+ years securing Discovery Bay homes, we've seen it all. But nothing prepares you for the creative destruction of pets. From cats who pick locks to dogs who deadbolt themselves in bathrooms, pet-proofing is a whole different security challenge. Let's explore the funny, frustrating, and utterly adorable world of pet security!

### **1. The "Feline Locksmith" Phenomenon**

**True Story:** Mittens, a Discovery Bay cat, learned to open round doorknobs by jumping and twisting. Her owners came home to find every interior door open and Mittens looking very pleased with herself.

**The Solution:** We installed **lever-style handles** throughout the house. "Cats can't get leverage on levers," we explained.

**The Outcome:** Mittens now opens the refrigerator instead. We're working on that.

### **2. The Dog Who Deadbolted Himself**

**The Scene:** Baxter, a golden retriever, was alone in the bathroom. Somehow, he managed to flip the deadbolt. His family spent 45 minutes coaxing him to turn it back.

**How It Happened:** Baxter was scratching at the door, his paw hit the deadbolt just right, and CLICK—he was prisoner in his own bathroom.

**Our Fix:** **Child-proof deadbolt covers.** They require human-level dexterity to operate. Baxter hasn't been incarcerated since.

### **3. The Parrot Who Knew the Alarm Code**

**The Discovery Bay Mystery:** A family's security alarm kept being disarmed. No signs of break-in. Nothing missing. Just the alarm turned off.

**The Culprit:** Polly, their African Grey parrot, had memorized the beep sequence and was mimicking it perfectly. The alarm system heard "beep beep beep beep" and thought someone had entered the code.

**Our Solution:** **Voice-command alarm systems** that require specific phrases. Polly now says "Secure the perimeter" instead, which is arguably more useful.

🐕 The Most Common Pet Security Issues in Discovery Bay

### **1. The "Let Me In/Let Me Out" Dilemma**

**Problem:** Pets constantly at doors wanting in or out

**Solution:** **Pet doors with locking mechanisms**

**Bonus:** Electronic pet doors that only open for your pet's microchip

### **2. The "I Ate Your Keys" Emergency**

**Problem:** Puppies love chewing keys

**Solution:** **Key hooks at puppy-proof height**

**Emergency Plan:** We keep records of your key patterns for quick replacement

### **3. The "Cat Burglar" Scenario**

**Problem:** Cats bringing "gifts" through pet doors

**Solution:** **Two-way pet doors** that only work coming in

**Discovery Bay Specific:** We've seen everything from fish to golf balls

### **4. The "Home Alone" Destruction**

**Problem:** Pets damaging doors trying to escape

**Solution:** **Reinforced door frames** and **scratch-resistant finishes**

**Pro Tip:** Security cameras to check on them remotely
